TAMIL NADU

The words Tamil Nadu literally means 'The Land of The Tamils or Tamil Country'. The state of Tamil Nadu is the 11th largest state out of 28 states of india. The Tamil nadu is known for so many things like temples,culture,food,movies,etc. Now we are going to see some of the famous temples in tamilnadu.

HERE ARE SOME OF THE FAMOUS TEMPLES IN TAMIL NADU

  1. KAPALEESWARAR TEMPLE
  2. BRIHADEESWARAR TEMPLE
  3. MEENAKSHI TEMPLE
  4. RAMANATHASWAMY TEMPLE

KAPALEESWARAR TEMPLE:

The Kapaleeswara temple in chennai is one of the popular temples in tamil nadu. The 7th century Amrirulgu Kapaleeswarar temple is dedicated to Goddess parvati in the form of Karpagambal or the 'Goddess of the Wish-Yielding Tree' and Lord Shiva.

The Gopuram,the main sanctuary in the temple is a unique example of Dravidian architectural style.The inscriptions in the premises of the temple date back to the 12th century.Major festivals like Brahmotsavam or 'Arupathumoovar' Festival,Theppa Thiruvizha Float Festival,Navaratri Festival, and Vasanta Utsavam are celebrated at the temple premises.

BRIHADEESWARAR TEMPLE:

Brihadeeswarar temple of Thanjavur is one of the best places to visit in Tamil Nadu. Popularly known as "Thanjavur Periya kovil",this is the largest temple in India. The outstanding architecture makes it one of the best temples in india.

The 1002-year old temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva.It was built in 1010 AD by the Chola Emperor,Rajaraja Chola 1.The temple represents the brilliancy and excellence of the architectural skills of cholas.There are 250 lingams and sculptures of his 108 dance poses in the temple premises.UNESCO has declared it a World Heritage Monument.

MEENAKSHI TEMPLE:

Madurai's Meenakshi Sundareswarar temple is one of the best temples in Tamil Nadu. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva in the form of Sundareswarar and his consort,Goddess parvati in the form of Meenakshi.The complex houses 14 magnificient Gopurams including two golden Gopurams for the main deities elaborately sculptured and painted.

The present temple standing today was built in the 17th century A.D. by the Nayak rulers.The image of Goddess Meenakshi is said to be carved out of a single emerald. According to the legend of this temple the marriage of the goddess Meenakshi to Shiva actually took place in Madurai and is still celebrated every summer with great excitement.

RAMANATHASWAMY TEMPLE:

Located in the Pamban Island.the Ramanathaswamy temple of Rameshwaram is one of the holiest temples in tamil nadu.The temple houses one of the 12 Jyotirlingas and is among the Char dham pilgrimages.

The Ramanathaswamy temple has the longest corridor in the world,about 3000 feet long. The templex is also home to 22 teerthams.or holy water bodies in the form of tanks and wells.These teerthams represent the 22 arrows in Rama's quiver.It is considered essential for every devotee to bathe before entering the sanctum sanctorum.The beautiful loacation and the divine ambience of Rameshwaram inspires visitors from all over the world throughout the year.
